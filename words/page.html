page

/peɪdʒ/

late 15th century: from French, from Latin pagina, from pangere ‘fasten’.

Definitions:

  • verb
    • look through the pages of (a book, magazine, etc.).
    • Computing
      • move through and display (text) one page at a time.
      • divide (a piece of software or data) into sections, keeping the most frequently accessed in main memory and storing the rest in virtual memory.
  • Noun
    • the material written or printed on a page.
    • a page of a newspaper or magazine set aside for a particular topic.
    • Computing
      • a section of stored data, especially that which can be displayed on a screen at one time.
    • a significant event or period considered as a part of a longer history.
